Methodology & Information

General Methodology

This calculator is based on the General Bearing Capacity Equation. This equation is a modified and expanded version of Terzaghi's classic formula, providing more accurate results by introducing a series of correction factors that account for various real-world conditions.

Primary References for Equations Used:
  • Vesic (1973, 1975): For bearing capacity factors (Nc, Nq, Nγ) and compressibility factors.
  • Hansen (1970): For depth factors (Fcd, Fqd).
  • De Beer (1970) / Meyerhof (1963): For shape factors (Fcs, Fqs, Fγs).

Key Assumptions

  1. Failure Mode: The soil is assumed to undergo general shear failure.
  2. Soil Homogeneity: Soil properties (c, φ) are considered constant and uniform below the foundation.
  3. Foundation Rigidity: The foundation is assumed to be rigid and act as a single unit.

Manual Calculation Verification Guide

To verify any result, follow these steps in order:

1. General Bearing Capacity Equation
qult = (c' Nc Fcs Fcd Fci Fcc) + (q Nq Fqs Fqd Fqi Fqc) + (0.5 γ' B' Nγ Fγs Fγd Fγi Fγc)
2. Correction Factor Definitions
Symbol Description
Fcs, Fqs, FγsShape factors, dependent on foundation dimensions (B/L).
Fcd, Fqd, FγdDepth factors, dependent on embedment depth to width ratio (Df/B).
Fci, Fqi, FγiLoad inclination factors, dependent on the angle of the load from the vertical axis.
Fcc, Fqc, FγcCompressibility factors, to account for soil rigidity.
3. Calculate Effective Vertical Stress `q` (Second Term)

This step depends on the location of the water table (Dw) relative to the foundation depth (Df).

Case A: Water table below foundation base (Dw ≥ Df)
q = γ₁ × Df
Case B: Water table above foundation base (Dw < Df)
q = (γ₁ × Dw) + ((γ₁_sat - γ_water) × (Df - Dw))
4. Calculate Effective Unit Weight `γ'` (Third Term)

This step depends on the water table location (Dw) relative to the failure zone below the foundation (which extends to depth Df + B').

Case A: Water is very deep (Dw ≥ Df + B')
γ' = γ₂
Case B: Water at or above foundation level (Dw ≤ Df)
γ' = γ₂_sat - γ_water
Case C: Water within the failure zone (Df < Dw < Df + B')

A linear average of the unit weight is used.
